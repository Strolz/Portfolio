Guido van Rossum

A Dutch programmer, Guido van Rossum not only created Python but also served as its “Benevolent Dictator For Life” (BDFL) until stepping down on July 12, 2018. Over his career, he worked at several major research institutions, including Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica (CWI) in the Netherlands and the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). From 2005 to 2012, Guido worked at Google, spending half his time improving the Python language. In 2013, he joined Dropbox, where he continued contributing to Python until his retirement in 2019. That retirement didn’t last long—he returned in 2020 to join Microsoft as a Distinguished Engineer, where he continues to influence the language he created. On a personal note, my biggest takeaway from researching Guido is his commitment, collaboration, and contribution to the world of development. The reach of his influence is truly astonishing.
